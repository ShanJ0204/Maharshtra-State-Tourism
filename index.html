Executive Summary

Maharashtra stands as a pivotal state in India's tourism landscape, characterized by its leadership in attracting international tourists while holding a strong, albeit not top-tier, position in the domestic market.

In 2023, Maharashtra welcomed 3.39 million foreign tourists, the highest among all Indian states, underscoring its global appeal and robust international connectivity, primarily through Mumbai [113]. Concurrently, it attracted 161.36 million domestic tourist visits, ranking it 5th nationally [144].

However, an evaluation of current marketing strategies and digital presence reveals critical gaps. While the "Maharashtra Unlimited" campaign aims to showcase diversity, the effectiveness of digital platforms, particularly social media, is suboptimal, with studies indicating a significant disconnect between tourist expectations and actual performance [4], [166].

Key Opportunities

  • Enhance digital marketing and social media engagement
  • Target niche markets: Chinese family travel, adventure, wellness
  • Strengthen partnerships and collaborations
  • Improve overall tourist experience and satisfaction

Maharashtra Tourism: Current Market Profile

Tourist Arrivals Overview

International Leadership

Maharashtra achieved a notable milestone by attracting 3.39 million foreign tourists in 2023, positioning it as the leading Indian state for international visitor arrivals [92], [113].

This represents around 17.6% of national FTAs in 2020 [114], largely attributed to Mumbai's status as a major international aviation hub [146].

Domestic Potential

With 161.36 million domestic tourist visits in 2023, Maharashtra secured the 5th rank among Indian states [144].

While substantial, this places Maharashtra behind Uttar Pradesh (478.53M), Tamil Nadu (286.01M), Karnataka (284.12M), and Rajasthan (179.05M) [144], indicating considerable growth potential.

Evaluation of Current Marketing Strategies

Maharashtra Unlimited Campaign

The Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation (MTDC) promotes the state under the "Maharashtra Unlimited" slogan [4], [17], highlighting diverse offerings from heritage sites to urban appeal.

The Tourism Policy of Maharashtra 2024 outlines a 360-degree marketing approach [5], including digital/online marketing, print/offline marketing, and "people marketing" through influencers.

Key Components

  • • Digital marketing (SEO, content, social media)
  • • Print/offline campaigns (airport displays, TV ads)
  • • Celebrity and influencer collaborations
  • • International event participation (5+ annually)
  • • Website and mobile app revamp

Digital Presence Challenges

Critical Performance Gaps

Studies reveal "huge gap (significant difference)" between tourist expectations and MTDC's social media performance [166].

Website evaluation shows substantial gaps in: [40]

  • • Quality of Information
  • • Customization and Interactivity
  • • Ease of Use
  • • Online Booking facilities

Visibility Issues

Researchers found it challenging to locate tourists who had visited MTDC's official social media pages, indicating severe visibility or engagement problems [166].

Identifying New Target Markets

Domestic Market Opportunities

Emerging Tier-Two Cities

Target travelers from Jaipur, Ahmedabad, Cochin, and Amritsar showing growing affluence and travel propensity [96].

Youth Market (Gen Z/Alpha)

50% of Airbnb guests in Ganpatipule were from Post-2000s demographic [100] - strong interest in adventure, wellness, and experiential travel.

Bleisure Travel

48% of Indian corporate travelers extend business trips for leisure [71]. Promote Mumbai, Pune, Nagpur as ideal bleisure destinations.

International Market Expansion

Established Markets

Top FTA sources: Bangladesh, US, UK, Australia, Canada (70.06% of total FTAs) [92]. US market grew from 13% to 18% share [91].

Chinese Family Market

80 million families considering outbound travel with children [60]. Prioritize educational value, culture, history - aligns with Maharashtra's offerings.

Emerging Markets

Sri Lanka, Malaysia, Germany, Nepal, France among top 10 sources [92]. France showed niche appeal in Ganpatipule (4% international guests) [100].
